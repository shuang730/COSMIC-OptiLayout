  <section>

    <h2>Designed for optical research</h2>

    <p class="section-text">

      COSMIC OptiLayout helps researchers quickly build, visualize, and modify 2D optical layouts.

      It supports interactive ray tracing, adjustable optical components, measurement tools, and figure export

      for experiment planning, discussion, reports, and presentations.

    </p>

  </section>


  <section>

    <h2>Features</h2>


    <div class="features">

      <div class="card">

        <h3>Real-time 2D Ray Tracing</h3>

        <p>Create and visualize optical paths interactively while editing the layout.</p>

      </div>


      <div class="card">

        <h3>Light Sources</h3>

        <p>Supports parallel beams and point sources with adjustable angle, beam width, ray count, and color.</p>

      </div>


      <div class="card">

        <h3>Optical Components</h3>

        <p>Includes lenses, objectives, mirrors, beam splitters, pinholes, cameras, and surfaces.</p>

      </div>


      <div class="card">

        <h3>Editable Parameters</h3>

        <p>Adjust position, rotation, size, focal length, numerical aperture, aperture, and other properties.</p>

      </div>


      <div class="card">

        <h3>Measurement Tools</h3>

        <p>Measure point-to-point and surface-to-surface distances directly in the optical layout.</p>

      </div>


      <div class="card">

        <h3>Export and Documentation</h3>

        <p>Export layouts as PNG or PDF for reports, presentations, and lab documentation.</p>

      </div>


      <div class="card">

        <h3>Productivity Tools</h3>

        <p>Includes save/load, undo/redo, box selection, alignment tools, zoom, pan, and view lock.</p>

      </div>


      <div class="card">

        <h3>Cross-platform Release</h3>

        <p>Precompiled macOS and Windows versions are available from GitHub Releases.</p>

      </div>

    </div>

    <h2>macOS Installation Note</h2>

    <p class="section-text">

      If macOS reports that <strong>MyAppInstaller_mcr.app</strong> is damaged and cannot be opened,

      this is caused by macOS Gatekeeper. The installer is not actually damaged.

    </p>


    <p class="section-text">

      Open Terminal and run:

    </p>


    <pre>xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine</pre>


    <p class="section-text">

      Then drag <strong>MyAppInstaller_mcr.app</strong> into the Terminal window to insert its path,

      press Enter, and launch the installer again.

    </p>
